# 10. Personal & Career-Specific

> **Why this section matters:** These are the questions that set the tone for the entire interview. Your opening narrative, career gap explanation, and "Why LSEG" answer will be evaluated within the first 10 minutes. Nail these and the rest of the interview flows from a position of strength.

---

### Q71: "Tell me about yourself."

**What's being evaluated:** Communication skills, self-awareness, ability to frame your professional identity concisely. Both interviewers will form a first impression from this answer.

**Answer guidance:**

Aim for 60–90 seconds. Follow the **Present → Past → Future** arc:

> "I'm a quality engineering professional who combines hands-on test automation expertise with strategic thinking. I've spent three and a half years building and leading test automation across complex enterprise platforms — SAP, Salesforce, and E-Commerce systems at Sysco LABS — where I designed Selenium and REST Assured frameworks, integrated them into CI/CD pipelines, and mentored junior engineers.
>
> After that intensive period, I made a deliberate investment in my education — completing a First Class MSc in Cloud Native Computing at TUS in Ireland and earning my ISTQB Advanced Level Technical Test Analyst certification. I also hold an MBA, which gives me a business perspective on quality — not just 'did the test pass,' but 'does this quality strategy serve the business.'
>
> Now I'm looking to bring that deeper foundation back to a challenging technical environment, and LSEG represents exactly that — global financial infrastructure where quality engineering has the highest stakes and greatest impact."

**Tips:**
- Practice this until it sounds natural, not rehearsed. Record yourself and listen back.
- Tailor emphasis per interviewer: with Amy, lean into the strategic/MBA/cloud-native angle; with Andy, emphasise the hands-on Selenium/REST Assured/CI/CD specifics.
- Do NOT list tools — that's a laundry list, not a narrative. Mention 2–3 tools max, embedded in context.
- Keep under 90 seconds. If they want more detail, they'll ask.

### Q72: "Walk us through the gap since October 2023."

**What's being evaluated:** Honesty, self-awareness, evidence of continued professional development. This is the single biggest risk area — how you handle it determines whether the interview continues on a positive trajectory.

**Answer guidance:**

Frame the gap as a **deliberate investment**, not an absence. Use the three-part structure: **what you did → why you did it → what it gave you**.

> "After three and a half years of intensive QA work at Sysco LABS, I made a deliberate decision to invest in my education. I moved to Ireland to complete my MSc in Cloud Native Computing at TUS, which I finished with First Class Honours in October 2024. This wasn't a step away from QA — it was a step deeper. The MSc covered Docker, Kubernetes, microservices architectures, and cloud-native design patterns — all directly relevant to how modern test infrastructure is built and deployed.
>
> During my time in Ireland, I also served as a Global Student Experience Assistant, which developed my cross-cultural communication skills — relevant for working across LSEG's global offices. I then completed my ISTQB Advanced Level Technical Test Analyst certification in April 2025, which deepened my expertise in test design techniques, white-box testing, and coverage analysis beyond what my work experience had covered.
>
> So while the timeline shows a gap in employment, the reality is continuous professional development — I returned with stronger technical foundations and broader perspectives than when I stepped away."

**What they're really evaluating (the subtext):**

| Their concern | Your counter |
|---|---|
| Are your skills current? | MSc in cloud-native computing (2024), ISTQB Advanced (2025) |
| Are you committed to QA? | Advanced certification + "Gen AI driven QA" as stated interest |
| Can you be productive quickly? | Cloud-native + advanced testing knowledge = faster ramp-up |

**Tips:**
- Be matter-of-fact, not defensive. If you sound apologetic, it signals you think it's a problem too.
- Never say "I was looking for the right opportunity" — this sounds passive.
- Don't pretend the Student Experience Assistant role was technical — it was a cross-cultural communication role. Own it for what it is.
- If they push further, mention specific coursework or projects from the MSc that relate to SDET work.

### Q73: "Why LSEG? Why this role?"

**What's being evaluated:** Research depth, genuine motivation, understanding of the company and role beyond the job description.

**Answer guidance:**

Structure around three pillars: **Scale & Impact → Technical Challenge → Growth & Culture**.

> "Three things draw me to LSEG specifically.
>
> First, **scale and impact**. LSEG operates critical global financial infrastructure — the quality bar isn't just 'no bugs,' it's 'billions of dollars of transactions depend on this working perfectly.' That level of stakes drives the kind of rigorous quality engineering I want to practice.
>
> Second, **technical challenge**. Testing real-time data delivery systems like LSEG Real-Time, across multiple platforms and regulatory environments, is a fundamentally different challenge from testing web applications. I want to grow into that complexity — and my MSc in Cloud Native Computing gives me the architectural foundation to contribute meaningfully to those systems.
>
> Third, **growth and culture**. LSEG's investment in AI-driven quality engineering, the global presence, and the engineering culture offer the kind of environment where I can both contribute and continue developing as a quality leader. I'm particularly drawn to the values — Integrity, Partnership, Excellence, and Change — because they align with how I approach quality: with rigour, collaboration, high standards, and a willingness to evolve."

**Tips:**
- Reference LSEG products by name: "LSEG Real-Time," "Workspace," "FTSE Russell indexes." This proves you've done your research.
- Connect your CA background: "My Chartered Accountancy studies give me a foundation in financial data that most SDET candidates lack."
- Show awareness of the Microsoft partnership and cloud migration efforts if you can reference recent news.
- Avoid generic answers like "It's a great company" — be specific about what excites you.

### Q74: "Where do you see yourself in 3–5 years?"

**What's being evaluated:** Ambition balanced with realism, commitment to the role and company, leadership trajectory (important for an "Associate Lead" title).

**Answer guidance:**

> "In 3 years, I see myself as a senior quality engineering practitioner — someone who shapes QA strategy for a product area, not just individual test suites. I'd like to be leading a QA stream, driving test automation architecture decisions, and mentoring the next generation of SDETs.
>
> In 5 years, I'd like to be influencing quality practices at an organisational level — perhaps driving initiatives like AI-augmented testing strategies or building quality metrics that connect directly to business outcomes. LSEG's scale and the complexity of financial markets infrastructure provides the right environment to develop those capabilities.
>
> The combination of my MBA, MSc, and hands-on QA experience gives me the foundation for that trajectory — but I know the financial domain expertise and the deeper platform knowledge come from doing the work, which is why this role is the right next step."

**Tips:**
- Balance ambition with humility — show you want to grow but recognise you have learning to do.
- Align your trajectory with LSEG's structure — don't describe a path that doesn't exist at the company.
- For Andy: emphasise the hands-on technical growth path (deeper framework design, cross-platform testing at scale).
- For Amy: emphasise the strategic leadership path (QA strategy, AI adoption, quality metrics).

### Q75: "Why are you leaving your current role? / What have you been doing recently?"

**What's being evaluated:** Motivation, honesty, whether you're running from something or toward something.

**Answer guidance:**

This is a softer variant of Q72 (the gap question). The key difference is tone — this question is about motivation, not explanation.

> "I'm not leaving a role — I'm completing a deliberate investment in my professional development. After my MSc and ISTQB Advanced certification, I'm now ready to return to a challenging technical environment where I can apply my deeper foundations.
>
> I chose to take time for education rather than staying in a comfort zone, because I saw the industry moving toward cloud-native architectures and AI-driven quality practices — and I wanted to be prepared for that shift, not catching up after it happened. Now I'm looking for the right environment to bring that investment to bear, and LSEG's combination of technical complexity, financial domain impact, and quality engineering maturity makes it exactly the right place."

**Tips:**
- Frame as "moving toward LSEG" not "moving away from a gap."
- If they mention the Student Experience Assistant role, be honest: "It supported me financially during my time in Ireland. My professional focus during that period was my MSc and certification studies."
- Keep this answer short — 60 seconds max. They'll probe further if needed.

### Q76: Questions to ask the interviewers

**What's being evaluated:** Preparation, genuine curiosity, strategic thinking, whether you understand the role and company at a deeper level.

**Answer guidance:**

Prepare 3–4 questions per interviewer. Choose based on conversation flow — don't robotically ask all of them.

#### Questions for Amy Lee (Director, Quality Engineering)

| # | Question | Why it works |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | "Your LinkedIn mentions AI-driven test automation — could you tell me more about LSEG's vision for AI in quality engineering? Where are you on that journey?" | Shows you've researched her. Opens her passion topic. Demonstrates forward-thinking mindset. |
| 2 | "How does the quality engineering team at LSEG measure success? What metrics matter most?" | Strategic question that shows you think beyond test execution. |
| 3 | "What does the career progression path look like for an SDET at LSEG? Where do you see this role leading in 2–3 years?" | Shows long-term commitment. Helps you understand actual role scope. |
| 4 | "How does the QE team collaborate with development teams? Is it embedded, centralised, or a hybrid model?" | Shows you understand different QA org models. |
| 5 | "What's the biggest quality challenge your team is currently facing?" | Problem-solving orientation. Gives you valuable intel. |

#### Questions for Andy Purtell (QE Manager — likely direct manager)

| # | Question | Why it works |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | "What does a typical day or sprint look like for an SDET on your team?" | Practical. Shows you care about day-to-day reality, not just the title. |
| 2 | "What tech stack does the team currently use for test automation? What are you considering adopting?" | Fills a critical info gap (JD doesn't specify). Shows practical readiness. |
| 3 | "You've been at LSEG and its predecessor organisations for a significant time — what keeps you here? What keeps the work interesting?" | Builds rapport. Gives genuine cultural insight. |
| 4 | "How large is the QA team I'd be joining? What's the team structure?" | Practical information. Shows you're thinking about how you fit in. |
| 5 | "What would success look like in the first 90 days for someone in this role?" | Shows you're already thinking about delivering value quickly. |
| 6 | "The role mentions supporting production releases and internal customers — how does that work in practice?" | Shows you read the JD carefully. Prepares you for full role scope. |

**Tips:**
- Pick 3–4 per interviewer based on conversation flow. Don't ask all of them.
- Don't ask about salary, benefits, or time off in the first interview.
- Don't ask questions easily answered by the JD or LSEG's website.
- Listen to their answers — ask follow-ups. The best interview questions lead to genuine conversation.
- If time is short, prioritise Q1 for Amy (AI vision) and Q5 for Andy (first 90 days).

### Q77: Tailoring your communication style to each interviewer

**What's being evaluated:** Emotional intelligence, adaptability, ability to communicate effectively with different stakeholders — a critical skill for an Associate Lead who works across teams.

**Answer guidance:**

This isn't a question they'll ask directly. It's a meta-strategy that should inform every answer you give.

#### Amy Lee — Director, Quality Engineering

| Dimension | Approach |
|---|---|
| **Level of detail** | Strategic. Lead with outcomes and business impact, not implementation details. |
| **Language** | "Quality strategy," "business value," "organisational capability," "risk reduction." |
| **What to emphasise** | MSc + MBA combination, leadership trajectory, AI/modern QA interest, global team experience. |
| **What to avoid** | Tool lists, overly tactical answers, "I tested X" without connecting to business impact. |
| **Her background** | Unix admin → Server management → QA leadership. Deep technical roots but now operates strategically. |
| **Key topics** | AI-driven test automation, quality metrics, team scaling, process maturity, career vision. |

**Example adaptation for Amy:**

Instead of: *"I used Selenium with TestNG and Page Object Model to automate 200 test cases."*

Say: *"I designed an automation strategy that reduced regression cycles from 5 days to 2 days, which directly enabled the team to release more frequently. The architecture used a layered approach — page objects for maintainability, data-driven patterns for coverage breadth — which I documented so the team could scale it independently."*

#### Andy Purtell — QE Manager (likely direct manager)

| Dimension | Approach |
|---|---|
| **Level of detail** | Specific. He wants to hear about real problems solved, real frameworks built, real bugs caught. |
| **Language** | "I built," "the specific challenge was," "the root cause turned out to be," "here's what I'd do differently." |
| **What to emphasise** | Hands-on coding, Selenium/REST Assured specifics, CI/CD integration details, debugging approach, mentoring specifics. |
| **What to avoid** | Vague or buzzword-heavy answers. He can detect superficial responses. |
| **His background** | QA Engineer → Senior QA → Tech Lead → QE Manager. Deep Elektron/real-time data experience. |
| **Key topics** | Framework architecture, flaky tests, cross-platform testing, test data management, team onboarding, production support. |

**Example adaptation for Andy:**

Instead of: *"I have experience with CI/CD integration."*

Say: *"At Sysco LABS, I set up Jenkins pipelines with parameterised builds — the commit stage ran smoke tests in under 5 minutes, and the full regression suite triggered nightly on the staging environment. We used SonarQube quality gates to block merges with code quality violations. One specific challenge was managing test data across environments — I solved that by building API-based data factories that created and cleaned up test data per suite execution."*

**Tips:**
- If both interviewers are in the room simultaneously, default to Andy's practical style but elevate key points to Amy's strategic level.
- Watch for cues — if Amy asks a follow-up, she wants more strategic depth. If Andy asks, he wants more technical detail.
- The dynamic between them: Amy screens for "Will this person grow into a quality leader?" Andy screens for "Can this person actually do the work?" You need to pass both filters.
