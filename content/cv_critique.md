## Executive Summary

This CV presents a QA professional with a solid technical foundation, strong academic credentials, and measurable achievements in test automation. However, when evaluated against the LSEG Associate Lead SDET role specifically, several gaps emerge — particularly around financial domain experience, infrastructure-level skills, and the breadth of employment history. ## Strengths

### 1. Quantified Achievements — The Standout Quality

The CV's biggest strength is its consistent use of concrete metrics. This is something many QA CVs fail at, and this one does it well:

- *"Reducing regression cycles by 45%"*
- *"Reduced regression execution time from 5 days to 2 days"*
- *"Automating 95% of the regression suite (200+ test cases)"*
- *"Improved test execution speed by 50%"*
- *"98% test coverage with 150+ automated test cases, identifying 20 critical defects prior to production release"*

These numbers give hiring managers something tangible to evaluate and demonstrate a results-oriented mindset. This is one of the most effective aspects of the CV.

### 2. Strong and Diverse Academic Credentials

The candidate holds three degrees, each adding a distinct dimension:

- **MSc in Cloud Native Computing (First Class Honours)** — Directly relevant to modern SDET work involving cloud-native systems, microservices, and containerised testing.
- **MBA** — Adds business acumen and strategic thinking, valuable for a lead-level role that requires aligning QA with business goals.
- **BSc (Hons) in Information Systems (Second Upper)** — Provides the technical foundation.

The combination of a technical MSc and an MBA is uncommon and positions the candidate well for roles that bridge technical execution and business stakeholder engagement.

### 3. Relevant and Current Certifications

Both certifications directly align with the LSEG role's expectation of *"shown expertise with software testing methodologies and tools."*

## Weaknesses

### 1. Significant Employment Gap and Career Continuity Concerns

This is the most critical issue on the CV:

- **Core QA experience ended in October 2023** — that is approximately 2.5 years ago.
- From **June 2024 to December 2025**, the candidate held a non-technical role (Global Student Experience Assistant), which involves event coordination and student support — not QA work.
- From **December 2025 to present (March 2026)**, there is an unexplained ~3-month gap.

For a fast-moving field like test automation, 2.5 years away from active QA work is a significant concern. Technologies, frameworks, and best practices evolve rapidly. The candidate should proactively address this — e.g., by highlighting any personal projects, open-source contributions, or continued learning during this period.

### 2. Inflated Experience Claim

The profile states *"over four years of experience."* However, examining the actual QA roles:

- **Software Quality Engineer:** March 2020 – July 2021 (~1 year 4 months)
- **Senior Software Quality Engineer:** August 2021 – October 2023 (~2 years 2 months)

This totals approximately **3 years and 6 months** of QA experience, all at the same company (Sysco LABS). The 6-month internship at eBuilder was in application support, not QA. The claim of "over four years" may be questioned by a diligent recruiter and could undermine credibility.

### 3. Single-Employer QA Experience

All QA experience comes from one company (Sysco LABS) working for one client (Brakes, UK). While the projects varied (Salesforce, SAP, E-Commerce), the context was a single outsourced engagement. This limits evidence of:

- Adapting to different organisational cultures and processes.
- Working with different codebases, architectures, and tech stacks.
- Navigating different team structures and development methodologies.

A hiring manager at LSEG, which is a global financial infrastructure company, may question whether this experience translates to their environment.

### 4. No Financial Domain Experience

The LSEG job description explicitly mentions *"some experience on financial markets"* as desirable. The CV shows:

- **Zero** mention of financial systems, trading platforms, market data, or financial regulations.
- The Chartered Accountancy (Business Level) qualification is listed but not connected to any practical financial domain application.
- No mention of FIX protocol, financial messaging standards, regulatory compliance testing, or similar domain-specific skills.

Given LSEG's core business is financial markets infrastructure, this is a notable gap. The candidate should at minimum acknowledge the CA qualification's relevance to understanding financial systems and express domain interest.

### 5. Missing Infrastructure and Systems Skills

The LSEG JD requires:

- *"Proficiency in Windows server and/or Unix/Linux"* — **Not mentioned at all** on the CV. Bash is listed under programming but server administration/proficiency is absent.
- *"Some experience on Networking, TCP/IP"* — **Not mentioned.** For an SDET role at a financial exchange, network-level testing knowledge is important.
- No mention of **containerisation** (Docker, Kubernetes) despite having an MSc in Cloud Native Computing — a surprising omission.
- No mention of **monitoring/observability tools** (Grafana, Splunk, ELK stack) which are common in SDET roles.

### 6. Performance Testing — Mentioned but Unsubstantiated

"Performance Testing" is listed under Methodologies, but:

- No specific performance testing tools are mentioned (JMeter, Gatling, k6, Locust).
- No performance testing achievements or metrics appear in the experience section.
- No mention of load testing, stress testing, endurance testing, or capacity planning.

For a financial markets SDET role where system performance is critical (think: trading latency, market data throughput), this gap is concerning. Either the candidate has this experience and it is not shown, or the skill is aspirational — both are problematic.

### 7. Missing CMM/CMMI and Formal SDLC Knowledge

The JD states: *"Experience of formal software development lifecycle and good knowledge on process such as CMM/CMMI."* LSEG, as a regulated financial entity, likely operates under formal process maturity models. The CV mentions Agile/Scrum but makes no reference to:

- CMM/CMMI frameworks.
- ISO standards (e.g., ISO 25010 for software quality).
- Formal audit or compliance processes for software quality.

### 8. Security Testing — Barely Addressed

One line mentions Veracode: *"Conducted security testing using Veracode."* This is thin. For a financial markets company, security is paramount. The CV should expand on:

- Types of security testing performed (SAST, DAST, penetration testing).
- Security vulnerabilities found and how they were addressed.
- Any knowledge of OWASP, security compliance standards.

### 9. Weak "Other Experience" Section for This Role

The three non-QA roles listed occupy significant CV real estate:

- **Global Student Experience Assistant** — event coordination, not technically relevant.
- **Teaching Assistant** — tangentially relevant (communication/mentoring) but adds limited value for an SDET lead role.
- **Application Support Engineer Intern** — the most relevant of the three, but from 2018-2019 and quite basic.

For a candidate competing for an Associate Lead SDET role, this space could be better utilised by expanding on technical achievements, side projects, or open-source contributions.

### 10. No Evidence of Database or Data-Centric Testing

SQL is listed as a skill, but the CV contains no examples of:

- Database testing or data validation strategies.
- ETL testing or data pipeline verification.
- Specific work with PostgreSQL or Oracle (both listed in Platforms).

In financial markets, data integrity testing is crucial, and this absence is a missed opportunity.

### 11. Interests Section is Underdeveloped

The interests section reads:

> - Gen AI driven QA and test automation
> - Building and mentoring high performance QA teams

These are professional interests, not evidence. The candidate should either:
- Remove this section entirely (it adds minimal value as stated), or
- Convert these into concrete evidence — e.g., specific AI-driven testing tools explored, or examples of team-building outcomes.

### 12. Missing Robot Framework, Perl, and C/C#

The JD specifically mentions: *"Shell/Python/Perl/Robot and/or C/C#/Java/VB."* The candidate covers Python, Java, and Shell/Bash, but:

- **Robot Framework** — not mentioned (widely used in enterprise SDET roles).
- **Perl** — not mentioned.
- **C/C#/VB** — not mentioned.

While not all languages are required, acknowledging awareness or willingness to learn could help.

## Structural and Formatting Observations

### Positives
- Clean, well-organised layout with clear section headers.
- Reverse chronological order for experience — standard and effective.
- Good use of bullet points with action verbs ("Designed," "Integrated," "Championed," "Led").
- Certifications and education are prominently placed and easy to scan.

### Areas for Improvement
- **CV length:** The content, if rendered as a traditional CV, may approach 3 pages. For a 3.5-year career in QA, a tighter 2-page CV would be more impactful.
- **Date formatting inconsistency:** Some dates use "August 2021" format, others would benefit from consistent formatting throughout.
- **No hyperlinks to projects or portfolios:** No GitHub, no portfolio, no links to automation frameworks or open-source contributions. For an SDET role, demonstrable code is valuable.
- **Bullet point density:** The Senior QA role at Sysco LABS has 11 bullet points. This is excessive. The top 6-7 most impactful points should be retained; the rest can be consolidated or removed.

## Recommendations for Improvement

1. **Address the career gap proactively.** Add a brief note about continued professional development during 2023-2026 (online courses, certifications, personal projects, etc.).

2. **Be precise about experience duration.** Say "approximately three and a half years" rather than "over four years" to maintain credibility.

3. **Add a "Financial Domain" angle.** Leverage the CA qualification more explicitly. Even a line about understanding financial reporting, accounting systems, or audit processes would help for LSEG.

4. **Add infrastructure skills.** If the MSc in Cloud Native Computing covered Docker, Kubernetes, Linux, or networking — mention these explicitly.

5. **Expand performance testing.** If there is any performance testing experience, detail it with tools and results. If not, consider gaining experience with JMeter or k6 and adding it.

6. **Add a GitHub or portfolio link.** Even a small personal project demonstrating automation framework design would significantly strengthen the application.

7. **Trim the "Other Experience" section.** Reduce the student assistant and teaching roles to 1-2 lines each, or move them to a brief "Additional Experience" line. Use the freed space for more relevant technical content.

8. **Consolidate bullet points.** Reduce the Senior QA role from 11 to 7 impactful bullets. Merge related points (e.g., combine the mentoring and workshop bullets).

9. **Add database and data testing examples.** Mention any SQL-based validation, data migration testing, or ETL verification work.

10. **Consider adding a "Technical Projects" section** highlighting the MSc dissertation or any significant automation framework built from scratch — this would help compensate for the limited employer diversity.

## Overall Assessment

**The CV presents a competent QA professional with genuine technical skills and strong academic credentials.** The quantified achievements and ISTQB Advanced certification are genuine differentiators. However, the combination of a 2.5-year gap from QA work, single-employer experience, and missing alignment with several LSEG-specific requirements (financial domain, infrastructure skills, performance testing, CMM/CMMI) creates a challenging profile for this particular role.

With targeted improvements — particularly addressing the gap narrative, adding infrastructure and domain-relevant skills, and tightening the overall presentation — this CV could be significantly strengthened for the LSEG opportunity.

**Fit Rating for LSEG Associate Lead SDET Role: 6/10**

The candidate has the right foundation and certifications, but the gaps in financial domain knowledge, infrastructure skills, and recent hands-on QA experience bring the fit score down. The academic credentials (MSc + MBA) and ISTQB Advanced certification are above-average for this level, which partially compensates.
